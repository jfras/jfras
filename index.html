Jan Fras, PhD candidate

I am PhD student at Queen Mary University of London at School of Engineering and Materials Science. I am member of Advanced Robotics at Queen Mary group (ARQ) led by prof. Kaspar Althoefer. My research focuses on soft robotics, in particular on soft actuation.
Alongside I am an engineer in Industrial Research Institute for Automation and Measurements PIAP where I work not only on soft hardware but also on autonomous behaviours, vision and human perception.

Projects

Soft pneumatic octopus

Swimming octopus robot. No rigit parts. Currently pnemuatic with external power source, but I work extensively to make it enclosed hydraulic device. Robot can swim straight, turn in any direction and twist along its primary axis.


Octopus' tentacle

The robot is driven by novel pneumatic actuator. It maximizes the actuation area while providing 2 degrees of freedom.


Soft prosthetic hand

Prosthetic hand made with soft materials only. It consist of exoskieleton made of less flexible silicone rubber that defines its motion and soft fluidical actuators housed in fingers. It provides 6 degrees of actuation as the thumb contains 2 separate sets of actuators that allow to bend and to reconfigure its orientation.



Soft industrial grippers

Pneumatic grippers for special tasks I developed for PIAP. They are soft and compiliant so suitable for fragile and delicate objects. They do not require any sophisticated control as they adapt to the handled objects. It has been design for mobile antiterrorst robots sold by PIAP.
